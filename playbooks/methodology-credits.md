# Methodology Credits

The sales frameworks, templates, and coaching methodology in this vault draw from the following sources.

## Champion-First Selling

**Source:** Nate Nasralla, "Selling With" and "Brief & Brilliant" methodology (Fluint.io)

**Key concepts used:**
- The business case IS the sales process (not a late-stage document)
- Champion readiness testing (change channel, assign action items, train them)
- SCQA structure for business cases (Situation, Complication, Question, Answer)
- 60-second deal review format (three headlines, evidence of buyer actions, deal health scores)
- Four-sentence soundbite (Because of / Now's the time / When they do / While avoiding)
- Discovery gap framework (Priority, Problem, Timing, Stakeholder)
- Driver graph analysis for building POVs
- Evidence of buyer actions (not seller activities) as the measure of deal progress

**Templates adapted from this methodology:**
- `_templates/60-second-deal-review.md`
- `_templates/1-page-business-case.md`
- `_templates/half-page-business-case.md`
- `_templates/mutual-action-plan.md`
- `_templates/executive-summary.md`

**Learn more:** fluint.io, "Selling With" by Nate Nasralla

## MEDDIC / MEDDPICC

**Source:** Industry standard qualification framework

**Key concepts used:**
- Metrics, Economic Buyer, Decision Criteria, Decision Process, Identify Pain, Champion, Competition
- Used in deal review templates for structured qualification

## Additional Influences

- Challenger Sale (CEB/Gartner) — teaching for differentiation, commercial insight
- SPIN Selling (Neil Rackham) — situation, problem, implication, need-payoff questioning
- Gap Selling (Keenan) — current state to future state gap analysis
