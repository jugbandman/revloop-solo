# Playbooks

Your sales process, documented. This is where YOUR way of doing things lives.

## What Goes Here

Write down the processes you actually follow. Not theory, not someone else's framework. How YOU do it.

**Examples:**
- `my-discovery-process.md` — How you run discovery calls, what you ask, what you listen for
- `our-sales-stages.md` — Your pipeline stages and what moves a deal forward
- `qualification-criteria.md` — How you decide if a prospect is worth pursuing
- `outreach-cadence.md` — Your sequence: timing, channels, messaging approach
- `deal-review-process.md` — How you review deals with your team or manager
- `meeting-run-of-show.md` — How you structure different meeting types

## Playbooks vs Knowledge

**playbooks/** = How I do it. My process, my way. Authored by me.

**knowledge/** = What I've learned from others. Frameworks, articles, posts, training materials. Collected from external sources.

The sales-coach agent reads both folders when coaching you on deals. Your playbooks give it context on YOUR process. Knowledge gives it the theoretical foundation.

## Getting Started

1. Pick your most-used sales motion (discovery, outreach, deal review)
2. Write down what you actually do, step by step
3. Don't make it perfect. A rough doc you use is better than a polished one you don't.
4. The sales-coach agent will reference these when coaching you on specific deals.
