# MEDDIC Qualification Framework

Enterprise deal qualification. Use this to assess whether a deal is real, winnable, and worth pursuing.

## The Elements

### M — Metrics
What measurable outcomes does the buyer expect?

**Key questions:**
- What KPIs are you measured on?
- What does success look like in numbers?
- What's the cost of the current problem (per month/quarter/year)?

**Red flags:** Vague outcomes ("better efficiency"), no quantified impact, can't tie to executive metrics.

### E — Economic Buyer
Who has the budget authority to approve this purchase?

**Key questions:**
- Who signs off on investments of this size?
- Have they been involved in the evaluation?
- What's their decision timeline?

**Red flags:** Only talking to influencers, economic buyer hasn't been identified, "committee decides."

### D — Decision Criteria
What criteria will they use to evaluate options?

**Key questions:**
- What are the must-haves vs nice-to-haves?
- How will you compare vendors?
- Are there technical requirements or compliance needs?

**Red flags:** No written criteria, criteria that perfectly match a competitor, criteria you can't meet.

### D — Decision Process
What steps do they follow to make a purchase?

**Key questions:**
- Walk me through how decisions like this typically get made here
- Who else needs to be involved?
- What's the procurement/legal process?

**Red flags:** Unknown process, "we'll figure it out," no defined timeline.

### I — Identify Pain
What specific pain is driving this evaluation?

**Key questions:**
- What triggered this evaluation now?
- What happens if you don't solve this?
- Who's most affected by this problem?

**Red flags:** No urgency, "exploring options," pain is aspirational not acute.

### C — Champion
Who inside the organization is actively selling on your behalf?

**Key questions:**
- Who benefits most from solving this problem?
- Will they advocate for this internally?
- Can they get you access to the economic buyer?

**Champion tests:**
1. Will they share information others won't?
2. Will they coach you on internal politics?
3. Will they present your business case to their executives?

**Red flags:** No internal advocate, champion is too junior, champion won't take action.

## MEDDPICC Extensions

### P — Paper Process
Legal, procurement, security review. Often the hidden 30-60 day delay.

### C — Competition
Who else are they evaluating? Including "do nothing" and "build internally."

## Scoring

For each element, score 1-5:
- **5:** Fully validated with evidence
- **3:** Partially known, needs validation
- **1:** Unknown or missing

A deal scoring below 3 on any element has a gap that needs addressing before it can be accurately forecasted.
