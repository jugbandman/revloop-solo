# Discovery Question Bank

Organized by what you're trying to learn. Pick 3-5 per meeting, not all of them. Listen more than you ask.

## Problem Questions
*What's broken, what it costs, who's affected.*

- What triggered this conversation / evaluation?
- What have you tried so far to solve this?
- What happened when that didn't work?
- Who on your team is most affected by this?
- What does this problem cost you per month/quarter? (In dollars, time, or missed opportunities)
- What happens if you don't solve this in the next 6 months?
- How long has this been a problem? What changed to make it urgent now?

## Priority Questions
*Where this sits relative to everything else on their plate.*

- Where does this rank among your top 3 priorities this quarter?
- Is this tied to a specific executive initiative or company goal?
- What's the compelling event or deadline driving this?
- If you could only fix one thing this quarter, would this be it?
- Who else in the organization cares about this problem?
- What gets pushed back if you pursue this?

## Process Questions
*How decisions get made here.*

- Walk me through how your organization typically evaluates and approves something like this
- Who else needs to weigh in on this decision?
- What does the procurement/legal process look like?
- What's a realistic timeline from "yes we want this" to signed agreement?
- Have you bought anything similar before? What did that process look like?
- Is there a budget allocated, or would this need to be carved out?

## Champion Questions
*Testing internal advocacy and access to power.*

- If you saw a solution that worked, who would you need to convince?
- Would you be comfortable presenting a recommendation to [executive]?
- What objections do you think [executive] would raise?
- Can you walk me through what a successful internal pitch looks like here?
- What would make you look good for bringing this forward?
- If I put together a one-page summary, would you be willing to share it with [decision maker]?

## Competitive Questions
*What else they're considering.*

- What other approaches are you evaluating?
- Have you considered doing this internally / building it yourselves?
- What would "do nothing" look like over the next year?
- What criteria are you using to compare options?
- Is there a preferred vendor or existing relationship that would have an advantage?

## Impact Questions
*Connecting the problem to measurable outcomes.*

- If this was solved, what would change for your team day-to-day?
- What metrics would improve? By how much?
- How would your executive measure success on this?
- What does the before/after look like in numbers?
- If I could show you [specific outcome], would that be enough to justify the investment?

## Questions to Avoid

- "What keeps you up at night?" (cliche, too broad)
- "What's your budget?" (too early, too blunt, earns distrust)
- "Are you the decision maker?" (offensive if they are, useless if they aren't)
- "When do you want to get started?" (assumptive, premature)
- Any question you could answer by reading their website

## How to Use This

1. Before a meeting, pick 3-5 questions based on what you DON'T know yet
2. Adapt the wording to sound natural in your voice
3. Ask open-ended, then follow up on what they say (don't just run through a list)
4. The best discovery question is the follow-up: "Tell me more about that" / "What do you mean by X?"
5. After the meeting, note which gaps you filled and which remain
